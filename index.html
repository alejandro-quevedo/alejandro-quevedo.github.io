Bio

I'm Alejandro, a Ph.D. Candidate in UC Santa Barbara's Electrical Engineering Department. I am currently researching LEDs and laser diodes with the Solid-State Lighting and Energy Electronics Center at UCSB. My research specifically involves long wavelength visible GaN LEDs (green, yellow, and red) and how to improve their wall-plug efficiency through V-defect engineering. I specialize in metal-organic chemical vapor deposition (MOCVD) crystal growth, device fabrication in UCSB's Nanofabrication Facility, and materials characterization. I also have expertise in LED packaging and the testing and electrical characterization of semiconductor devices. On my current research project I am advised by Prof. Steven DenBaars, Prof. James S. Speck, and Prof. Shuji Nakamura.

I have been heavily involved with teaching during my graduate studies. The courses I have TA'd and lectured for are listed below. I was also fortunate to recieve an Outstanding ECE TA Award in Spring 2022.

I graduated from UCSB in 2019 with a B.S. in Physics and Mathematical Sciences. Before returning for graduate studies, I worked as an Application Engineer for Sage Millimeter (now Eravant), a mm-wavelength RF company. I recieved my M.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering with an emphasis in Electronics and Photonics in 2022 and am currently working towards my PhD.
